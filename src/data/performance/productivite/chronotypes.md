# Le Mythe des Lève-Tôt et des Couche-Tard

## Science des Chronotypes


Science: You are naturally productive at different times of the day depending on your circadian rhythm—the part of your brain that tells every cell in your body when to be active and inactive.
Whether you are a morning person or an evening person is a basic indicator of your underlying circadian rhythm. It's called your chronotype, and we use it to define your preference for earlier or later sleep time and wake time. For simplicity, we often group chronotypes into morning people and evening people. However, science measures them on a continuous scale. In a recent study, researchers found 351 genetic variations associated with being a morning person. Your exact chronotype is specific to your genetics, age, and exposure to environmental light. The Center for Environmental Therapeutics provides an online survey to help you find and understand your chronotype.
